The Salt Lake Temple is Located in Salt Lake City, Utah. It is the sixth temple the church has completed, and the largest of them all at 253,015 square feet. The temple was dedicated in 1893, taking 40 years to complete. The temple stands, on 10 acres, as a centerpiece of Temple Square. At the east end of the building, on the center pinnacle, stands a statue of the andel Moroni.

The location for the temple was given by Brigham Yound on July 28, 1847, just 4 days after arriving in the Salt Lake Valley. The temple site was dedicated on February 14, 1853 by Heber C Kimball. And the cornerstone was laid later that year on April 6th.

The temple, which is used by Latter-Day Saints, is considered to be the house of God. Uses for the temple include an endowment ceremony, washing and anointing ceremonies, marriage, and baptismal ceremonies.
